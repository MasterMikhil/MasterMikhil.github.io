The Rise of Electric Vehicles

The electric vehicle (EV) revolution began with small, incremental steps.
This timeline tracks key moments in the development of electric cars and the global push towards sustainability.

January 2010
Tesla introduces its first electric car, the Roadster, showing the world the potential of electric vehicles with a range of over 200 miles.
March 2015
The Chevy Bolt EV is launched, providing an affordable alternative to Tesla's luxury offerings, and pushing the industry closer to mass-market electric vehicles.
July 2020
Governments around the world, including the EU and the US, announce ambitious goals for the phase-out of gas-powered vehicles, further accelerating the electric vehicle transition.

Countries like Norway plan to ban the sale of new internal combustion engine vehicles by 2025, promoting EV adoption as a critical environmental initiative.

February 2021
Tesla achieves a milestone of producing over 1 million vehicles, cementing its position as the leader in the electric vehicle market.

FAQs
What is The Great Ninja Hack?
The Great Ninja Hack is a 24-hour open innovation hackathon designed to challenge participants' creativity, technical skills, and problem-solving abilities. It offers a competitive platform where individuals or teams can develop innovative solutions and compete for a ₹50,000 prize pool.
When and where is the hackathon taking place?
The hackathon will take place from 22nd to 23rd February 2025. The event starts at 10:00 AM on 22nd February and runs continuously for 24 hours.
How can I participate in the hackathon?
Participants must register between 12th February 2025 (6:00 AM) and 17th February 2025 (12:00 PM). Additionally, teams need to submit their idea presentations before the registration deadline. Shortlisted teams will be announced on 18th February 2025 at 12:00 PM.
What is the prize for the winning teams?
The total prize pool for The Great Ninja Hack is ₹50,000, which will be awarded to top-performing teams based on their project quality and innovation.
What is the theme of the hackathon?
The hackathon follows an Open Innovation theme, allowing participants to work on any creative and impactful idea that aligns with real-world problem-solving.
